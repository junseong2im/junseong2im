## Research Publications

### PhysSim: Physics-Simulated Molecular Interactions Predict Olfactory Perception Without Pretrained Models

A physics simulation engine that predicts olfactory mixture similarity by evolving molecular embeddings under four fundamental forces (gravity, Coulomb, van der Waals, spin-orbit coupling) with learnable constants. Achieves SOTA on Snitz 2013 (Spearman rho = 0.613) without pretraining, outperforming GNNs and attention-based models.

[![Paper](https://img.shields.io/badge/Paper-PDF-blue)](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15001664/v1) [![ChemRxiv](https://img.shields.io/badge/ChemRxiv-Preprint-orange)](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15001664/v1) [![Code](https://img.shields.io/badge/Code-GitHub-black)](https://github.com/junseong2im/PhysSim-Olfactory-Mixture-Similarity)

| Benchmark | PhysSim | Best Baseline | p-value |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Snitz 2013 (CV) | rho = 0.613 | 0.602 (Attention Mix) | 0.050 |
| Ravia 2020 (zero-shot) | rho = 0.408 | 0.206 (RDKit cos) | - |
| Bushdid 2014 (cross-task) | rho = -0.492 | -0.454 (RDKit cos) | - |

### UMN-GR: General Relativistic N-body Simulation for Modeling Olfactory Mixture Perception

A general relativistic framework that models molecular mixtures as massive bodies in curved spacetime, using Schwarzschild-inspired geodesic equations to simulate inter-molecular interactions for olfactory similarity prediction.

[![Paper](https://img.shields.io/badge/Paper-PDF-blue)](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15001285/v1) [![ChemRxiv](https://img.shields.io/badge/ChemRxiv-Preprint-orange)](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15001285/v1) [![Code](https://img.shields.io/badge/Code-GitHub-black)](https://github.com/junseong2im/UMN-GR-Modeling-Olfactory-Mixture-Perception-via-General-Relativistic-N-body-Simulation)
